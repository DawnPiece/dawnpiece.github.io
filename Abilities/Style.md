# Style

Players will be able to choose a style that they will like to play as in game. To begin with, players will have access to choose any style to start with, and leveling up will allow you to unlock more abilities to use in the future. Unlockable abilities will be stronger at their base value.

### Style Leveling

Style leveling is tied to the amount of experience points by the player when using any style move. Experience points are tied to damage dealt for damage moves, damage recieved for armor moves, health regeneration for heal moves, distance traveled for speed moves, and so on and so forth.<br>

Each style will have its own level save data, so if a player changes their style, they start the new style at level 0. If they change back to a style they used before, then they return to that style's level save data. Styles will require more experience points to level up as you progress. The max level is 60 for each style.<br>

### Skill Points

You can access the DawnPiece Main Menu in game by clicking the M keybind by default. From there click the Skill Tree option and you can see all of the skills available to upgrade your current active style. Each skill will start at Level 1 and have the potential to be upgraded to Level 5. Each upgrade costs more skill points than the last, and each ability will require a total of 10 Skill Points to max each one out. As there are 42 abilities for each style, reaching Level 60 will give you 420 Skill Points alowing you to max out every ability in that Skill Tree.

Every ability level up will increase damage, protection, range or other positive stats depending on the move. For example, a damage move will start with it's original damage based on its level, and when it reaches level 5 it will do 100% of the damage. Every level up will also decrease cooldown, debuffs, and other negative stats depending on the move. For example, a move may have a cooldown of 15 seconds and when its level 5 it drops to 5 seconds.

### Style Progression Lines

There will be 8 style progression lines that will be geared towards certain weapons of your choice, and as a result will buff their respective weapon damage output. Each style line will have its own progression system, so switching between styles will have seprate leveling systems and skill trees. The following style lines are below.

1. [Swordsman](https://haneki56.github.io/DawnPiece/#/Abilities/Style/Swordsman): Focused on Slash Weapon Related Styles
2. Bruiser: Focused on Bludgeon Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
3. Brawler: Focused on Impact Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
4. Scout: Focused on Pierce Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
5. Binder: Focused on Pull Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
6. Glider: Focused on Float Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
7. Gunslinger: Focused on Ranged Weapon Related Styles (Planned)
8. Martial Artist: Focused on Weaponless Related Styles (Planned)
